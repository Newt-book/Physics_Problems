# Galactic Collision

## Given

Distance between the Milky Way and Andromeda:

\[
d = 2.5 \text{ million light-years}
\]

Speed of Andromeda towards the Milky Way:

\[
v = 110 \text{ km/s}
\]

We need to estimate the time until collision.

---

# Step 1 — Start from the basic speed formula

Speed is defined as distance travelled per unit time:

\[
v=\frac{d}{t}
\]

To find time, rearrange the formula.

Multiply both sides by \(t\):

\[
vt=d
\]

Now divide both sides by \(v\):

\[
t=\frac{d}{v}
\]

So the formula we need is:

\[
t=\frac{\text{distance}}{\text{speed}}
\]

---

# Step 2 — Convert light-years to kilometres

The distance is given in light-years, but the speed is in kilometres per second.

So we must convert light-years into kilometres.

One light-year is the distance light travels in one year:

\[
1 \text{ light-year} = 9.46\times10^{12} \text{ km}
\]

Therefore:

\[
2.5 \text{ million light-years}
=
2.5\times10^6 \text{ light-years}
\]

Now multiply:

\[
d = 2.5\times10^6 \times 9.46\times10^{12}
\]

\[
d = 23.65\times10^{18}
\]

\[
d = 2.365\times10^{19}\text{ km}
\]

---

# Step 3 — Substitute into the time formula

\[
t=\frac{d}{v}
\]

\[
t=\frac{2.365\times10^{19}}{110}
\]

\[
t=2.15\times10^{17}\text{ s}
\]

So the time is:

\[
2.15\times10^{17}\text{ seconds}
\]

---

# Step 4 — Convert seconds into years

One year is approximately:

\[
1\text{ year}=365\times24\times60\times60
\]

\[
1\text{ year}=31,536,000\text{ s}
\]

\[
1\text{ year}=3.1536\times10^7\text{ s}
\]

Now divide the time in seconds by the number of seconds in one year:

\[
t=\frac{2.15\times10^{17}}{3.1536\times10^7}
\]

\[
t=6.82\times10^9\text{ years}
\]

---

# Final Answer

\[
\boxed{t\approx6.8\times10^9\text{ years}}
\]

Therefore, the estimated time until collision is approximately:

\[
\boxed{6.8\text{ billion years}}
\]

---

# Explanation

This is only an estimate. We assumed that Andromeda moves directly towards the Milky Way at a constant speed of \(110\text{ km/s}\). In reality, gravity and the motion of both galaxies make the actual situation more complicated.
