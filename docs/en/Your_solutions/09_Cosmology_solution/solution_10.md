# Cosmic Microwave Background

## Given

Current temperature of the CMB:

\[
T_{\text{now}} = 2.7\text{ K}
\]

Temperature during recombination:

\[
T_{\text{recombination}} = 3000\text{ K}
\]

Wien’s displacement constant:

\[
b = 2.898\times10^{-3}\text{ m K}
\]

Wien’s displacement law:

:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

We want to determine:
1. The peak wavelength during recombination,
2. The peak wavelength today.

---

# Step 1 — Understanding Wien’s Law

Wien’s displacement law states that the wavelength of maximum radiation emitted by a blackbody is inversely proportional to its temperature.

This means:
- hotter objects emit radiation with shorter wavelengths,
- cooler objects emit radiation with longer wavelengths.

---

# Step 2 — Peak Wavelength During Recombination

Substitute:

\[
T = 3000\text{ K}
\]

into Wien’s law:

\[
\lambda_{\text{peak}}
=
\frac{2.898\times10^{-3}}{3000}
\]

Calculate:

\[
\lambda_{\text{peak}}
=
9.66\times10^{-7}\text{ m}
\]

Convert to nanometers:

\[
1\text{ nm}=10^{-9}\text{ m}
\]

\[
\lambda_{\text{peak}}
=
966\text{ nm}
\]

---

# Step 3 — Interpretation

A wavelength of approximately:

\[
966\text{ nm}
\]

lies in the infrared region of the electromagnetic spectrum, very close to visible red light.

This means that during recombination, the universe emitted radiation mainly in the infrared region.

---

# Step 4 — Peak Wavelength Today

Now use the present CMB temperature:

\[
T=2.7\text{ K}
\]

Substitute into Wien’s law:

\[
\lambda_{\text{peak}}
=
\frac{2.898\times10^{-3}}{2.7}
\]

\[
\lambda_{\text{peak}}
=
1.073\times10^{-3}\text{ m}
\]

Convert to millimeters:

\[
1\text{ mm}=10^{-3}\text{ m}
\]

\[
\lambda_{\text{peak}}
=
1.07\text{ mm}
\]

---

# Step 5 — Interpretation

A wavelength of approximately:

\[
1.07\text{ mm}
\]

belongs to the microwave region of the electromagnetic spectrum.

This explains why it is called the:

\[
\text{Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB)}
\]

---

# Final Answers

### Peak wavelength during recombination

\[
\boxed{
\lambda_{\text{peak}}
\approx9.66\times10^{-7}\text{ m}
}
\]

or

\[
\boxed{
966\text{ nm}
}
\]

---

### Peak wavelength today

\[
\boxed{
\lambda_{\text{peak}}
\approx1.07\times10^{-3}\text{ m}
}
\]

or

\[
\boxed{
1.07\text{ mm}
}
\]

---

# Physical Explanation

As the universe expanded after the Big Bang, the radiation stretched to longer wavelengths through cosmological redshift.

As a result:
- the temperature decreased from approximately \(3000\text{ K}\) to \(2.7\text{ K}\),
- and the peak wavelength shifted from infrared wavelengths to microwave wavelengths.
